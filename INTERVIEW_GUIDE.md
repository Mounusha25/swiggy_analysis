## 🎤 Interview Talking Points

### For Technical Screens

**Q: "Walk me through a challenging analytics project you've done."**

*Answer Framework:*
1. **Business Context:** "I analyzed Swiggy food delivery data to identify growth opportunities and revenue optimization strategies..."

2. **Technical Approach:** "I started with exploratory data analysis - checking data quality, handling missing values, and identifying outliers using IQR method. Then I performed statistical tests including correlation analysis and normality testing..."

3. **Key Findings:** "Using Pareto analysis, I discovered 20% of cities drove 80% of revenue. I also segmented customers into 4 tiers and found premium customers had higher satisfaction but lower volume..."

4. **Impact & Recommendations:** "I recommended focusing marketing on top-performing cities and launching loyalty programs for high-value segments..."

### Technical Deep-Dive Questions

**Q: "How did you handle outliers?"**
- "I used box plots and IQR method (Q1 - 1.5*IQR, Q3 + 1.5*IQR) to detect outliers"
- "Found X% of orders were outliers - high-value catering orders"
- "Rather than removing them, I analyzed separately as they represented growth opportunities"

**Q: "What statistical tests did you perform?"**
- "Correlation analysis between price and rating (found weak correlation r=0.XX)"
- "Normality testing using Q-Q plots and computed skewness/kurtosis"
- "Distribution analysis showing right-skewed pricing patterns"

**Q: "How did you approach forecasting?"**
- "Implemented simple moving averages (3-month and 6-month)"
- "Calculated month-over-month growth rates to identify trends"
- "Used trend lines to project next quarter revenue"

### For Behavioral Interviews

**STAR Method Examples:**

**Situation:** "The food delivery company wanted to optimize their geographic expansion strategy..."

**Task:** "My goal was to analyze sales data across all cities and identify high-potential markets..."

**Action:** "I performed Pareto analysis, geographic clustering, and customer segmentation. Built interactive dashboards showing city-level performance..."

**Result:** "Identified top 10 cities for expansion based on revenue per capita and growth trends. Recommendations could drive 15% revenue increase..."

## 💡 Common Interview Questions & Answers

**Q: "What was the most interesting insight?"**
*A:* "The Pareto analysis revealing 20% of cities drive 80% revenue was eye-opening. It showed we should focus resources on high-performing markets rather than spreading thin."

**Q: "What would you do differently?"**
*A:* "I'd add more advanced forecasting like ARIMA models, build automated pipelines for real-time updates, and incorporate external factors like weather, events."

**Q: "How did you ensure data quality?"**
*A:* "Systematic approach: checked for nulls, duplicates, outliers. Validated data types. Cross-referenced totals. Documented all cleaning steps."

**Q: "How do you communicate technical findings to non-technical stakeholders?"**
*A:* "I created an executive summary with clear visualizations, avoided jargon, focused on business impact (revenue, growth), and provided actionable recommendations."
